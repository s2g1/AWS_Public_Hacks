## SECTION 1 – GENERAL INFORMATION

### 1.1 Purpose

The Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA), Tactical Technology Office (TTO), hereby solicits proposals from qualified offerors for the research, development, integration, and demonstration of an Autonomous Drone Swarm Battlefield Mapping System (ADSBMS) capable of operating in denied and contested environments under Emissions Control (EMCON) conditions.

This solicitation is issued under Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) Part 15, Contracting by Negotiation, and is subject to all applicable provisions of the FAR, DFARS, and agency supplements.

### 1.2 Background

Modern battlefield environments present increasingly complex challenges to intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance (ISR) operations. Adversarial electronic warfare (EW) capabilities, GPS denial, and anti-access/area denial (A2/AD) systems degrade or eliminate reliance on traditional ISR assets. The Government requires a novel, autonomous approach to rapid battlefield mapping that is resilient to these threats.

Existing manned and single-platform unmanned aerial systems (UAS) solutions are insufficient for the required coverage area, timeline, and survivability parameters. A coordinated swarm approach utilizing inter-agent coordination and AI-driven path planning offers a promising path to overcoming these limitations.

### 1.3 Procurement Strategy

## SECTION 2 – STATEMENT OF WORK / PERFORMANCE WORK STATEMENT

### 2.1 Scope of Work

The Contractor shall design, develop, integrate, test, and demonstrate a production-representative Autonomous Drone Swarm Battlefield Mapping System (ADSBMS) capable of deploying and coordinating a swarm of 15 to 20 autonomous drones to generate an accurate, real-time battlefield common operating picture (COP) across a 10 square kilometer area of operations within a 45-minute mission window.

All operations shall be performed in compliance with EMCON Level II conditions, which prohibit active emissions from swarm assets unless specifically authorized by mission parameters. The system shall demonstrate resilience against representative electronic warfare threats including GPS jamming, RF signal denial, and directed energy spoofing.

### 2.2 Technical Requirements

#### 2.2.1 Swarm Configuration and Autonomy
- Minimum 15, maximum 20 heterogeneous UAS platforms
- Fully autonomous mission execution (waypoint upload through post-mission consolidation)
- Dynamic re-tasking for platform attrition, sensor degradation, or obstacles
- Consensus-based inter-agent communication (no single point of failure)
- Onboard AI/ML inference at ≥10 Hz update rate

#### 2.2.2 Mapping and Sensor Performance
- Georeferenced orthomosaic at ≥15 cm GSD
- ≤3 meters CEP under GPS-denied conditions
- Preliminary battlefield picture within 45 minutes of deployment
- EO sensor baseline; provisions for SAR/LIDAR modular payloads

#### 2.2.3 EMCON and Electronic Warfare Resilience
- Pre-planned routes, optical coordination, or acoustic mesh during EMCON
- GPS jamming detection/response within 5 seconds
- Anti-spoofing measures for all navigation inputs
- LPI/LPD waveforms when RF authorized

#### 2.2.4 Ground Control and Integration
- Windows 10/11 compatible GCS, MIL-STD-461 compliant hardware
- Real-time swarm status + 4 simultaneous video downlinks
- NATO STANAG 4586 conformance
- NITF, GeoTIFF, KMZ export (ATAK compatible)
